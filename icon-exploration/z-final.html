Zealova · App Icon — the Z as a shape

Two takes where the Z isn't a letter

Not the Z styled. The Z turned into something — so you read a shape first and find the letter second. That is the move in Gravl and Hevy. Shown big, then at 60 px and 40 px.

Split
Two interlocking forms — the Z is the gap
A solid orange tile cleaved by a dark river into two chunky interlocking shapes. The Z is the negative space between them, not a drawn letter. You see two forms lock together; the Z arrives a beat later. The FedEx-arrow move.

My honest read — after rendering them

Split works. It is the best mark in this whole exploration. The Z stopped being a letter you read — it is the negative-space river cleaving one bold tile into two interlocking forms. You read a designed shape first, the Z second. It holds at 40 px and sits credibly next to Hevy and Gravl. This is the one.

Fold did not land — the three shades read as a tricolour Z, not a convincing folded object. That illusion needs real shadow craft; flat planes do not get there.

If Split is right, I refine it — balance the two halves, tune the river width and elbow radius, try a few proportions — then export to PNG and wire it into the iOS + Android icon sets.
