Zealova — Icon Exploration · MOMENTUM

Three letterless abstract marks. The brief: express forward / upward momentum and measurable transformation without the chevron, arrow, or lightning-bolt cliche. Each is one chunky, multi-readable shape on near-black, built as an extractable <symbol>. Flat solid greens only.

CONCEPT 01 Crest

A solid wave frozen at the instant of breaking — a heavy mass pitched forward at its peak, the curl already thrown ahead of the base. It is momentum made into a sculpture: a wave, a body cresting a jump, a growth curve that has gained mass. Asymmetric and right-leaning, so it always reads as moving forward.

READS AT 60PX

Mostly. The two-tone break holds and the silhouette is one bold blob. Risk: at 40px the curl can flatten into a generic leaf — the forward lean survives, the "wave" specificity weakens.

COLLISION CHECK

Searched "wave logo app icon", "abstract wave fitness logo", "motion blur teardrop logo". Waves are common in surf / water / spa brands but rare as a chunky two-tone fitness mark; no direct collision. Stays clear of arrow + bolt families.

CONCEPT 02 Quicken

One bold form cut into three leaning plates. The acceleration is carried by spacing and scale — each plate is shorter, lighter and thrown further forward than the last — so the eye reads a single object speeding up, not a static bar chart. The deep-to-bright palette makes the motion also a build of energy: week over week, faster.

READS AT 60PX

Yes — three high-contrast leaning slabs survive cleanly and the rhythm reads. Honest risk: three separated plates is structurally a "progress bars" layout; it is closer to a known visual idiom than the others, even though the shear and unequal gaps push it past a plain chart.

COLLISION CHECK

Searched "ascending steps logo fitness app", "accelerating shapes speed lines logo", "three bars logo". The literal staircase idiom is heavily used and was rejected; this skewed-plate variant has no direct brand collision but lives near a common pattern.

CONCEPT 03 Surge

Two chunky comma-blades swept the same rotational direction — a heavy trailing blade and a smaller, lighter blade thrown out ahead of it. The blades are deliberately unequal and offset, not a symmetric pinwheel, so the figure has one clear direction and reads as a kinetic form accelerating. The pair also quietly carries Zealova's two-into-one story. Negative near-black between the blades does the work.

READS AT 60PX

Adequate. The two-blade silhouette and the spin direction hold, and the unequal blades keep it from collapsing into a symmetric loop. Honest risk: at 40px the gap between the blades narrows and the form can read as a single rounded swoosh rather than two blades — the two-part idea is the first thing to soften.

COLLISION CHECK

Searched "two halves merging logo abstract", "vortex pinwheel spiral logo app icon", "yin yang fitness logo". Symmetric pinwheels and infinity loops are common stock tropes; the unequal-blade, single-direction sweep deliberately breaks that symmetry to dodge both. No direct brand collision found.

Recommendation — Concept 01, "Crest", is the strongest. It is the only one of the three that is a genuinely single, indivisible shape: Quicken is three pieces a viewer can still read as a chart, and Surge is two blades that can soften into one swoosh at small sizes. Crest is one chunky asymmetric mass with the forward lean and the forward-thrown curl built into its silhouette, it owns the green, and it sidesteps every item on the avoid list. It is not as conceptually loaded as Surge and not as instantly "motion" as a chart-like Quicken, which is an honest trade, but it is the most ownable and the most robust mark at 60px.
