The Z, abstracted

Three takes that try to do what Gravl and Hevy do — push the letter until it reads as a shape first: chunky weight, abstracted form, the negative space doing work. Not a typeset Z. Shown big, then at 60 px and 40 px on a home screen.

Bolt
The Z pushed until it reads as a lightning bolt — chunky, angular, sharp terminals, leaned forward. A Z and a bolt are nearly the same shape; this leans all the way in. Energy = "zeal".

My honest read — after rendering them

Bolt is the one. It stopped being a typeset letter — it is a chunky, angular lightning bolt that happens to be a Z, with real energy ("zeal" turned into a shape). The strongest Z-mark in this whole exploration, and genuinely shippable.

Facet is competent but I did not land the Gravl move — it is a Z sitting inside a hexagon, not a mark where the letter and the shape are one idea. Twin did not work: it reads as a broken Z, not two intentional forms.

Pick Bolt and I refine it — tune the lean, balance the weight, sharpen the terminals, give you 2-3 variations — then export to PNG and wire it into the iOS and Android icon sets.
