Research on creatine monohydrate for resistance training:

Creatine monohydrate is the most studied ergogenic aid in sports science. A 2017 ISSN position stand reviewed over 700 peer-reviewed studies and concluded that 3-5g per day produces consistent improvements in maximal strength (5-15%) and high-intensity work capacity (10-15%) across resistance-trained individuals.

Key practical points:

1. **No loading phase needed**: While a 20g/day loading week saturates muscle creatine faster (about 6 days vs. 4 weeks), the end-state benefit is identical. Most coaches now skip the load to avoid GI side effects.

2. **Timing is largely irrelevant**: Studies comparing pre- vs. post-workout dosing find no meaningful difference. Daily consistency matters more than time of day.

3. **Water retention is real but small**: Expect a 1-2 lb intramuscular water gain in the first month. This is not subcutaneous and does not affect body composition meaningfully.

4. **Hair loss claim is overblown**: The single often-cited study (van der Merwe et al., 2009) measured DHT in rugby players for 3 weeks; no follow-up study has replicated the result, and population-scale evidence is absent.

5. **Kidney safety**: Meta-analyses of healthy individuals using creatine for up to 5 years show no renal impact. Pre-existing kidney disease is the only contraindication worth flagging.

Sources: Kreider et al., JISSN 2017; Antonio et al., 2021; Forbes et al., 2022.
