**One-Pan Chicken Tikka Masala (Serves 4)**

A weeknight-friendly version of the takeout classic. Skip the long marinate; the spices still deliver.

**Ingredients**
- 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
- 3 tablespoons garam masala
- 1 tablespoon ground turmeric
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1 (15 oz) can crushed tomatoes
- 1 cup heavy cream (or full-fat coconut milk)
- Fresh cilantro, chopped
- Cooked basmati rice or naan, for serving

**Instructions**
1. In a large bowl, whisk yogurt with 2 tablespoons garam masala, turmeric, cumin, paprika, and salt. Toss chicken to coat. Marinate at least 15 minutes (or up to 24 hours).
2. Heat ghee in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken in two batches, about 3 minutes per side, until lightly browned. Transfer to a plate.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion to the same pan and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in garlic and ginger; cook 1 minute.
4. Add remaining 1 tablespoon garam masala and cook 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in crushed tomatoes; simmer 8 minutes.
5. Stir in heavy cream and return chicken to the pan. Simmer 5 more minutes, until chicken is cooked through (165°F internal).
6. Taste and adjust salt. Garnish with cilantro and serve over rice or with warm naan.

**Macros per serving (recipe makes 4)**
- 480 kcal · 35g protein · 18g carbs · 30g fat
