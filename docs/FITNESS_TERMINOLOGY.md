# Fitness & Gym Terminology Guide

A comprehensive glossary of terms used by gym-goers, fitness professionals, and athletes. Each term includes whether it should be integrated into the AI Fitness Coach app.

---

## PERFORMANCE METRICS

### 1RM (One Rep Max)
**Definition**: The maximum weight you can lift for a single repetition with proper form.
**Example**: "My 1RM for bench press is 225 lbs"
**App Integration**: YES - Critical for workout programming. Used to calculate working weights (e.g., "Do 5 reps at 75% of your 1RM")

### PR / PB (Personal Record / Personal Best)
**Definition**: Your all-time best performance for a specific exercise or activity.
**Example**: "I hit a new PR on deadlift today - 315 lbs!"
**App Integration**: YES - Track and celebrate PRs. Great for gamification and sharing.

### Rep (Repetition)
**Definition**: One complete movement of an exercise from start to finish.
**Example**: "Do 10 reps of squats"
**App Integration**: YES - Fundamental to all workout tracking.

### Set
**Definition**: A group of consecutive reps performed without rest.
**Example**: "Do 3 sets of 10 reps"
**App Integration**: YES - Core workout structure element.

### Volume
**Definition**: Total amount of work done. Calculated as Sets x Reps x Weight.
**Example**: "3 sets x 10 reps x 100 lbs = 3,000 lbs volume"
**App Integration**: YES - Important for tracking progressive overload and weekly workload.

### Tempo
**Definition**: The speed at which you perform each phase of a rep. Written as 4 numbers (eccentric-pause-concentric-pause).
**Example**: "3-1-2-0 tempo" means 3 sec down, 1 sec pause, 2 sec up, 0 sec at top
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced feature. Could add for intermediate+ users.

### Time Under Tension (TUT)
**Definition**: Total time your muscles spend under load during a set.
**Example**: "Increase TUT by slowing down your reps"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced metric for hypertrophy-focused users.

### RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)
**Definition**: A 1-10 scale measuring how hard an exercise feels. 10 = absolute max effort, couldn't do another rep.
**Example**: "That set was RPE 8" (could have done 2 more reps)
**App Integration**: YES - Great for autoregulating workouts based on daily readiness.

### RIR (Reps in Reserve)
**Definition**: How many more reps you could have done before failure.
**Example**: "Stop at RIR 2" (stop when you have 2 reps left in the tank)
**App Integration**: YES - Alternative to RPE, easier for beginners to understand.

### AMRAP (As Many Reps As Possible)
**Definition**: Perform maximum reps until failure or near-failure.
**Example**: "Final set is AMRAP"
**App Integration**: YES - Common in workout programming for testing/pushing limits.

### EMOM (Every Minute On the Minute)
**Definition**: Start a new set every minute, rest is whatever time remains.
**Example**: "EMOM 10: 5 burpees" (do 5 burpees, rest until next minute, repeat 10x)
**App Integration**: YES - Popular conditioning format.

### HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)
**Definition**: Alternating between intense bursts of activity and rest periods.
**Example**: "30 seconds sprint, 30 seconds rest"
**App Integration**: YES - Already in the app. Core workout type.

### LISS (Low-Intensity Steady State)
**Definition**: Sustained low-intensity cardio for extended periods.
**Example**: "45 minutes of walking at 3.5 mph"
**App Integration**: YES - Important for recovery and fat loss phases.

### VO2 Max
**Definition**: Maximum oxygen consumption during exercise. Measures cardiovascular fitness.
**Example**: "Her VO2 max is 45 ml/kg/min"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Requires external device (Apple Watch). Could integrate via HealthKit.

## TRAINING CONCEPTS

### Progressive Overload
**Definition**: Gradually increasing stress on muscles over time to continue making gains.
**Example**: "Add 5 lbs to your squat each week"
**App Integration**: YES - Critical. App should auto-suggest weight increases.

### Periodization
**Definition**: Systematic planning of training phases to optimize performance and prevent plateaus.
**Example**: "4-week strength block, then 4-week hypertrophy block"
**App Integration**: YES - AI should plan training phases based on goals.

### Deload
**Definition**: A planned reduction in training volume/intensity to allow recovery.
**Example**: "Every 4th week is a deload week - 50% volume"
**App Integration**: YES - Prevent overtraining. Auto-schedule deloads.

### Supercompensation
**Definition**: The body adapts stronger than before after recovering from training stress.
**Example**: "Rest properly to hit supercompensation before next session"
**App Integration**: NO - Too technical. Handle behind the scenes in programming.

### Mind-Muscle Connection
**Definition**: Consciously focusing on the muscle being worked to improve activation.
**Example**: "Really squeeze your biceps at the top"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Could include as tips in exercise instructions.

### Training to Failure
**Definition**: Performing reps until you physically cannot complete another rep.
**Example**: "Take the last set to failure"
**App Integration**: YES - Important concept. Mark sets as "to failure" in workouts.

### Drop Set
**Definition**: Immediately reducing weight after reaching failure and continuing reps.
**Example**: "Do 10 reps, drop 20%, do 8 more, drop 20%, do 6 more"
**App Integration**: YES - Advanced technique. Include in workout options.

### Superset
**Definition**: Two exercises performed back-to-back with no rest between.
**Example**: "Superset: Bicep curls + Tricep extensions"
**App Integration**: YES - Common technique. Support in workout builder.

### Giant Set
**Definition**: Three or more exercises performed back-to-back with no rest.
**Example**: "Giant set: Squats > Lunges > Leg Press > Leg Extensions"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced. Consider for future update.

### Compound Set
**Definition**: Two exercises for the same muscle group performed back-to-back.
**Example**: "Compound set: Bench Press + Dumbbell Flyes (both chest)"
**App Integration**: YES - Include in workout options.

### Tri-Set
**Definition**: Three exercises performed back-to-back targeting the same muscle group.
**Example**: "Tri-set for shoulders: Front raise, lateral raise, rear delt fly"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Can be covered by Giant Set feature.

### Rest-Pause
**Definition**: Take brief rest (10-15 sec) mid-set to squeeze out more reps.
**Example**: "Do 8 reps, rest 10 sec, do 3 more, rest 10 sec, do 2 more"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced technique for later.

### Cluster Set
**Definition**: Breaking a set into mini-sets with short rest to accumulate more volume at heavy weights.
**Example**: "5x2 with 15 sec rest instead of 1x10"
**App Integration**: NO - Too advanced for most users.

### Pyramid Set
**Definition**: Increasing or decreasing weight with each set.
**Example**: "100 lbs x 12, 120 lbs x 10, 140 lbs x 8, 160 lbs x 6"
**App Integration**: YES - Popular structure. Support in workout builder.

### Reverse Pyramid
**Definition**: Starting with heaviest weight and decreasing.
**Example**: "160 lbs x 6, 140 lbs x 8, 120 lbs x 10"
**App Integration**: YES - Efficient for strength. Include as option.

### Forced Reps
**Definition**: Partner assists to complete reps beyond failure.
**Example**: "Get 2-3 forced reps with your spotter's help"
**App Integration**: NO - Requires partner. Not applicable for solo app.

### Negatives / Eccentric Training
**Definition**: Focusing on the lowering portion of a lift with slow, controlled movement.
**Example**: "5-second negatives on pull-ups"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Could add tempo support.

### Pause Reps
**Definition**: Adding a pause at the bottom or top of a rep to increase difficulty.
**Example**: "Pause 2 seconds at the bottom of each squat"
**App Integration**: YES - Good for strength. Add pause timer option.

### Cheat Reps
**Definition**: Using momentum or other muscles to lift heavier than strict form allows.
**Example**: "Use a little body English on the last few reps"
**App Integration**: NO - Don't encourage. Focus on proper form.

## MUSCLE & BODY TERMS

### Hypertrophy
**Definition**: Muscle growth through training.
**Example**: "I'm in a hypertrophy phase - 8-12 reps per set"
**App Integration**: YES - Goal type. Optimize workouts for muscle growth.

### Atrophy
**Definition**: Muscle loss from lack of use or undertraining.
**Example**: "Avoid atrophy by training consistently"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Use for motivation when user misses workouts.

### DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness)
**Definition**: Muscle soreness that peaks 24-72 hours after exercise.
**Example**: "The DOMS from leg day is brutal"
**App Integration**: YES - Ask about soreness to adjust next workout intensity.

### Pump
**Definition**: Temporary muscle swelling during/after exercise due to blood flow.
**Example**: "Got an insane pump today"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Fun metric. "Rate your pump" after workout?

### Gains
**Definition**: Progress made in muscle size, strength, or performance.
**Example**: "Making solid gains this month"
**App Integration**: YES - Use in celebratory messages and progress tracking.

### Natty / Natural
**Definition**: Someone who doesn't use performance-enhancing drugs.
**Example**: "I'm 100% natty"
**App Integration**: NO - Don't need to address PEDs in the app.

### Cutting
**Definition**: Phase focused on losing fat while maintaining muscle.
**Example**: "I'm cutting for summer - caloric deficit"
**App Integration**: YES - Training phase/goal. Adjust workouts and nutrition advice.

### Bulking
**Definition**: Phase focused on gaining muscle with caloric surplus.
**Example**: "Bulking season - eating 500 calories over maintenance"
**App Integration**: YES - Training phase/goal. Adjust programming.

### Lean Bulk / Clean Bulk
**Definition**: Bulking with minimal fat gain through controlled surplus.
**Example**: "Lean bulk with 200 calorie surplus"
**App Integration**: YES - More nuanced goal option.

### Dirty Bulk
**Definition**: Bulking by eating anything to maximize calorie intake.
**Example**: "Dirty bulk - pizza and burgers every day"
**App Integration**: NO - Don't encourage unhealthy eating.

### Recomp (Body Recomposition)
**Definition**: Simultaneously losing fat and gaining muscle.
**Example**: "I'm doing a recomp - weight stays same, body changes"
**App Integration**: YES - Common goal, especially for beginners.

### Maintenance
**Definition**: Eating/training to maintain current weight and muscle.
**Example**: "Taking a maintenance phase between cuts"
**App Integration**: YES - Important phase option.

### Shredded / Ripped / Lean
**Definition**: Very low body fat with visible muscle definition.
**Example**: "He's absolutely shredded at 8% body fat"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Use in goal visualization or achievements.

### Jacked / Swole
**Definition**: Having significant muscle mass.
**Example**: "You're looking jacked bro"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Fun achievement badge names.

### Lagging Body Part
**Definition**: A muscle group that develops slower than others.
**Example**: "My calves are a lagging body part"
**App Integration**: YES - AI could identify and suggest focus work.

### Mind-Muscle Connection
**Definition**: Focusing mentally on the muscle being worked.
**Example**: "Really feel your lats during rows"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Include as coaching tips.

## WORKOUT SPLITS & PROGRAMS

### Bro Split
**Definition**: Training one muscle group per day (chest day, back day, etc.).
**Example**: "Monday chest, Tuesday back, Wednesday shoulders..."
**App Integration**: YES - Common split option.

### PPL (Push/Pull/Legs)
**Definition**: Split dividing workouts into pushing, pulling, and leg movements.
**Example**: "PPL 6 days: Push, Pull, Legs, Push, Pull, Legs, Rest"
**App Integration**: YES - Very popular. Already likely in app.

### Upper/Lower Split
**Definition**: Alternating between upper body and lower body days.
**Example**: "Upper Monday, Lower Tuesday, Upper Thursday, Lower Friday"
**App Integration**: YES - Great for intermediates.

### Full Body
**Definition**: Training all major muscle groups each session.
**Example**: "Full body 3x per week"
**App Integration**: YES - Best for beginners.

### Arnold Split
**Definition**: Chest/back, shoulders/arms, legs - 6 days per week.
**Example**: "The classic Arnold split for bodybuilding"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Named program option.

### 5x5
**Definition**: 5 sets of 5 reps, typically for compound lifts.
**Example**: "Starting Strength uses 5x5 for squats"
**App Integration**: YES - Classic strength protocol.

### 5/3/1
**Definition**: Jim Wendler's popular strength program with monthly progression.
**Example**: "Running 5/3/1 for my main lifts"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Could add as preset program.

### German Volume Training (GVT)
**Definition**: 10 sets of 10 reps for extreme hypertrophy.
**Example**: "10x10 squats at 60% 1RM"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced program option.

### Conjugate Method
**Definition**: Westside Barbell's system with max effort and dynamic effort days.
**Example**: "Conjugate for powerlifting"
**App Integration**: NO - Too complex for app scope.

### Linear Progression
**Definition**: Adding weight every session or week.
**Example**: "Add 5 lbs to squat each workout"
**App Integration**: YES - Core progression method for beginners.

### Undulating Periodization
**Definition**: Varying rep ranges throughout the week.
**Example**: "Monday heavy (5 reps), Wednesday medium (8), Friday light (12)"
**App Integration**: YES - Good intermediate programming.

## EXERCISE TERMINOLOGY

### Compound Exercise
**Definition**: Multi-joint movement working multiple muscle groups.
**Example**: "Squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows"
**App Integration**: YES - Tag exercises as compound. Prioritize in workouts.

### Isolation Exercise
**Definition**: Single-joint movement targeting one muscle group.
**Example**: "Bicep curls, leg extensions, lateral raises"
**App Integration**: YES - Tag exercises. Use for accessory work.

### Accessory Work
**Definition**: Supplementary exercises supporting main lifts.
**Example**: "Tricep work as accessory for bench press"
**App Integration**: YES - AI should suggest relevant accessories.

### The Big 3 / Big 4
**Definition**: Squat, Bench, Deadlift (+ Overhead Press for Big 4). The main compound lifts.
**Example**: "Focus on getting your Big 3 numbers up"
**App Integration**: YES - Core lifts. Track separately.

### Functional Training
**Definition**: Exercises mimicking real-world movements.
**Example**: "Farmer's walks, Turkish get-ups"
**App Integration**: YES - Workout style option.

### Calisthenics
**Definition**: Bodyweight-only exercises.
**Example**: "Push-ups, pull-ups, dips, pistol squats"
**App Integration**: YES - Equipment-free workouts.

### Plyometrics
**Definition**: Explosive jumping/bounding exercises.
**Example**: "Box jumps, jump squats, burpees"
**App Integration**: YES - Include in athletic/HIIT workouts.

### Isometric
**Definition**: Holding a position without movement.
**Example**: "Plank, wall sit"
**App Integration**: YES - Include with timer support.

### Concentric
**Definition**: The lifting/shortening phase of a movement.
**Example**: "Curling the weight up"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - For tempo training.

### Eccentric
**Definition**: The lowering/lengthening phase of a movement.
**Example**: "Lowering the weight slowly"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - For tempo training.

### ROM (Range of Motion)
**Definition**: Full movement path of a joint during exercise.
**Example**: "Use full ROM on squats - ass to grass"
**App Integration**: YES - Mention in exercise instructions.

### Lockout
**Definition**: Full extension at the top of a movement.
**Example**: "Lock out your elbows at the top of bench"
**App Integration**: YES - Include in form cues.

### Sticking Point
**Definition**: The hardest part of a lift where you're most likely to fail.
**Example**: "My sticking point on bench is mid-chest"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced coaching.

### Form
**Definition**: Proper technique during an exercise.
**Example**: "Keep strict form on your rows"
**App Integration**: YES - Critical. Form tips for every exercise.

### Spotter
**Definition**: Person who assists/protects during heavy lifts.
**Example**: "Get a spotter for max attempts"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Remind users for heavy lifts.

### Warm-Up Set
**Definition**: Light sets before working weight to prepare muscles.
**Example**: "2 warm-up sets before your working sets"
**App Integration**: YES - Auto-generate warm-up sets.

### Working Set
**Definition**: The main sets at your target weight.
**Example**: "After warm-ups, do 4 working sets"
**App Integration**: YES - Core workout structure.

### Back-Off Set
**Definition**: Lighter sets after heavy work for extra volume.
**Example**: "After your heavy 3x3, do a back-off set of 8-10"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced feature.

### Feeder Set
**Definition**: Light sets to pump blood before heavy work.
**Example**: "A couple feeder sets to get the muscles ready"
**App Integration**: NO - Too nuanced.

## NUTRITION TERMS

### Macros (Macronutrients)
**Definition**: Protein, carbohydrates, and fats.
**Example**: "Hitting my macros: 180P/300C/70F"
**App Integration**: YES - If adding nutrition tracking.

### TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)
**Definition**: Total calories burned in a day.
**Example**: "My TDEE is about 2,500 calories"
**App Integration**: YES - Calculate for nutrition recommendations.

### BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)
**Definition**: Calories burned at complete rest.
**Example**: "BMR is the minimum your body needs to function"
**App Integration**: YES - For calorie calculations.

### Caloric Deficit/Surplus
**Definition**: Eating less/more than TDEE.
**Example**: "500 calorie deficit for 1 lb/week loss"
**App Integration**: YES - For cutting/bulking recommendations.

### Protein Timing
**Definition**: When you eat protein relative to workouts.
**Example**: "30g protein within 1 hour post-workout"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - If adding nutrition features.

### Anabolic Window
**Definition**: Supposed optimal period post-workout for protein intake (largely debunked for most people).
**Example**: "Get your protein in the anabolic window"
**App Integration**: NO - Outdated concept.

### Pre-Workout
**Definition**: Supplement or meal taken before training.
**Example**: "Taking my pre-workout 30 mins before"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Reminder feature.

### Post-Workout / Post
**Definition**: Nutrition after training.
**Example**: "Protein shake for my post"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Reminder feature.

### Refeed
**Definition**: Planned higher-calorie day during a cut.
**Example**: "Refeed on Sundays to restore glycogen"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced nutrition feature.

### Carb Cycling
**Definition**: Varying carb intake based on training days.
**Example**: "High carbs on leg day, low carbs on rest days"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced nutrition feature.

## RECOVERY TERMS

### Active Recovery
**Definition**: Light activity on rest days to promote recovery.
**Example**: "Light walk or yoga for active recovery"
**App Integration**: YES - Suggest active recovery workouts.

### Passive Recovery
**Definition**: Complete rest.
**Example**: "Full rest day, no training"
**App Integration**: YES - Schedule rest days.

### Sleep Gains
**Definition**: Muscle growth and recovery that happens during sleep.
**Example**: "Get 8 hours for optimal sleep gains"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Sleep tracking integration.

### CNS Fatigue (Central Nervous System)
**Definition**: Exhaustion from heavy/intense training that goes beyond muscular fatigue.
**Example**: "Feeling fried - CNS is taxed"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Factor into deload recommendations.

### Overtraining
**Definition**: Excessive training without adequate recovery, leading to performance decline.
**Example**: "Watch out for overtraining symptoms"
**App Integration**: YES - Track volume and warn users.

### Overreaching
**Definition**: Short-term excessive training that leads to supercompensation after rest.
**Example**: "Planned overreaching before a deload"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Advanced programming concept.

### Mobility
**Definition**: Ability to move joints through full range of motion.
**Example**: "Working on hip mobility for deeper squats"
**App Integration**: YES - Include mobility work in warm-ups.

### Flexibility
**Definition**: Muscle length and ability to stretch.
**Example**: "Stretching for flexibility"
**App Integration**: YES - Include stretch routines.

### Foam Rolling / SMR (Self-Myofascial Release)
**Definition**: Using a foam roller to release muscle tension.
**Example**: "Foam roll your IT band"
**App Integration**: YES - Include in recovery recommendations.

### Gym Bro
**Definition**: Stereotypical gym enthusiast, often focused on aesthetics.
**Example**: "Just gym bro things"
**App Integration**: MAYBE - Use in fun AI coach personality.

### Broscience
**Definition**: Gym advice not backed by scientific evidence.
**Example**: "That's pure broscience"
**App Integration**: NO - Avoid perpetuating myths.

### Newbie Gains
**Definition**: Rapid progress beginners make when starting training.
**Example**: "Enjoy the newbie gains while they last"
**App Integration**: YES - Explain to new users why progress is fast initially.

### Plateau
**Definition**: Period where progress stalls despite training.
**Example**: "Hit a plateau on my bench press"
**App Integration**: YES - Detect and suggest solutions.

### Ego Lifting
**Definition**: Lifting too heavy with bad form to impress others.
**Example**: "Don't ego lift - drop the weight and fix your form"
**App Integration**: YES - AI coach should discourage this.

### Leg Day
**Definition**: Workout day dedicated to legs.
**Example**: "Never skip leg day"

### Arm Day
**Definition**: Workout day for biceps and triceps.

### Chest Day

### Beast Mode

### No Pain No Gain

### Gymtimidation

### Swole

### Shred / Get Shredded

**App Integration**: YES - Goal type.

## SUMMARY: APP INTEGRATION PRIORITY

### Must Have (Implement First)
- 1RM / PR / PB
- Reps, Sets, Volume
- RPE / RIR
- AMRAP, EMOM, HIIT
- Progressive Overload
- Deload
- Supersets, Drop Sets
- Compound vs Isolation
- Full Body, PPL, Upper/Lower splits
- Form guidance
- Warm-up sets
- Cutting, Bulking, Recomp goals
- DOMS tracking
- Plateau detection
- Newbie gains explanation
- Gymtimidation support

### Should Have (Phase 2)
- Tempo training
- Time Under Tension
- Pyramid sets
- LISS cardio
- Periodization phases
- Mobility work
- Active recovery
- Overtraining warnings
- Macros (if adding nutrition)
- TDEE/BMR calculations

### Nice to Have (Phase 3+)
- VO2 Max integration
- Mind-muscle connection tips
- Rest-pause, Cluster sets
- Named programs (5/3/1, GVT)
- Sleep tracking integration
- CNS fatigue tracking
- AR trophy achievements with slang names

### Don't Include
- Broscience terms
- Cheat reps encouragement
- Dirty bulk advice
- Forced reps (requires partner)
- PED-related terms
- Outdated concepts (anabolic window)
