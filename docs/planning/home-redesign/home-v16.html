Zealova — What the "+" does

Keeping it as a floating button (not the nav centre). Here's exactly what it does — and how it stops covering content.

What the "+" button does

The "+" is the universal quick-logger. Tap it from anywhere and a Quick Log sheet slides up (phone 2) — log Food, Water, Weight, a Workout, Measurements, or Mood in one or two taps, plus an AI input at the top: type or speak "2 eggs and a coffee" and it logs the meal for you.

Why it exists alongside the quick-action row: the 6 quick actions live at the top of Home and scroll away. The "+" floats and follows you — it's on Home, Workouts, Nutrition, every tab — so logging is always one tap, wherever you are. The quick-action row = shortcuts; the "+" = the always-there logger.

Fixing the "it covers content" problem (without the nav centre)

Per your call it stays a floating button. To stop it burying tiles: it auto-hides while you scroll down and slides back when you scroll up or stop — so when you're reading the bento it tucks away; when you pause, it's there. The page also keeps bottom padding so the last tile never sits under it. That's the standard Material behaviour and it removes the overlap you saw.
