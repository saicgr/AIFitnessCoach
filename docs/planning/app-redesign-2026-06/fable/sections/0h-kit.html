KIT 1/4Food logging — the access path, demonstrated
The loop answers "how do I get to it" instead of assuming it: the frame starts as the plain nutrition screen with the composer ("Log food, water, anything…") prominent at the bottom; a tap ripple lands on it, the screen dims, and the dark sheet rises — composer-tap → sheet, watched rather than described. Inside, the describe-first field (the typewriter alternates "salmon poke bowl…" and "had 500ml water" — water logs through the same path), six modes as hairline chips — every Cal AI camera mode (Scan Food, Barcode, Food Label) plus first-class Scan Menu, voice and saved; Cal AI's multi-modal logging with its correction loop is the category benchmark, and Zealova runs corrections in the same conversational thread. Alternate entries: the Home quick-actions sheet via the global "+", or the composer's 📷/▥/🎤 glyphs jumping straight into that mode.
Risk: six modes in two chip rows risks clutter — order must follow real usage frequency (describe and scan first), the grid must never grow a third row, and correction-after-log must stay one tap, not a re-log.

KIT 2/4Micros — pinned panel + full explorer
MacroFactor's Nutrient Explorer — 54 tracked nutrients, up to 8 pinned to the dashboard — is the micros benchmark, and this keeps Zealova's /nutrition/micros at full parity: eight pinned hairline tiles with thin orange fill bars, a quiet full-panel ledger beneath, and the 54-nutrient depth one tap away — restyled into the editorial dark ledger instead of a data dump. Sodium's amber bar shows semantic color spent only where it means something.
Risk: percent-of-target bars imply a precision the food data rarely has — sparse-entry days need confidence states, and the amber must read as a heads-up, never a scold.

KIT 3/4Custom trends — any two metrics, kept
The /trends/custom power feature survives whole: overlay any two metrics, swap them in place, read the correlation — now in the editorial chart voice. Hairline grid, Barlow axis micro-labels, orange × cyan lines drawing in, and the correlation spoken in the coach's Fraunces serif instead of a stats tooltip. Saved pairs keep their r-values as quiet ledger rows.
Risk: r-values need guardrails — small samples and confounders make spurious correlations easy, so below a data threshold the serif line should hedge ("early signal") rather than assert causation.

KIT 4/4Food detail — provenance kept
Tap any food row anywhere — Today, recents, search — and it opens this: the floating 3D render as hero, a dotted tap-to-edit serving line, the Anton calorie poster with semantic macro dots, and a micros mini-row one tap from the full panel. The part most apps throw away is first-class here: how the entry was logged (text, photo, menu scan, barcode, label) and the original photos are stored data, surfaced as the provenance chip and thumbnails — nothing the current app records is dropped in the redesign.
Risk: photo thumbnails of real user food sit next to the stylized 3D render — two visual languages on one screen; the thumbnails must stay small, square and quiet (and expandable on tap) or the hero loses its authority.
