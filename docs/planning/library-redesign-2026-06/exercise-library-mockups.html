Exercise Library — Signature v2 Directions

The Exercise Library is a sub-screen of the Workouts tab, surfacing a catalog of ~1,500 exercises across strength, cardio, core, plyometric and mobility work. Below: the current screen rendered as-is with an honest critique, then ten distinct layout paradigms — each a real, shippable iOS direction re-skinned to Zealova's signature v2 system (tinted near-black, Anton display, Barlow labels, hairline rows, orange as the single accent).

★Recommended

Lead with #4 Quick-Action Rows — it kills the buried-actions problem and ties the library straight into today's workout. Graft in #2 Muscle-Map Browse as an alternate entry and #9 Faceted Filter Bar for power narrowing. #1 Visual Poster Grid is the runner-up for a more image-forward feel.

Visual Poster Grid

Best for: visual browsers who recognize moves by sight, not name.

  • Muscle + difficulty legible at a glance per tile.
  • Feels premium and scrollable, like a board of lifts.

  • Answers "what hits my chest?" with zero typing.
  • Visually teaches muscle groups while you browse.

Best for: active planners building a workout while they browse.
  • Kills the buried-actions problem — favorite/add in one tap.
  • Difficulty stripe reads faster than a text label.
  • Swap action ties the library directly to today's workout.

Collections / Playlists

A collection-forward home — orange-edged cards for "Your performed", "Favorites", "Push-day staples", "Bodyweight only", plus made-for-you sets and a "Browse all". Spotify for exercises.

Best for: returning users who curate and re-use their own sets.
  • Personal collections become the front door, not a buried filter.
  • Playlist metaphor makes a 1,500-catalog feel owned, not vast.
  • "Browse all" stays one tap away for full-catalog dives.
