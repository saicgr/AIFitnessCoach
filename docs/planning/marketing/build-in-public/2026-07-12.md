I didn't post here for 7 weeks.

Not because I stopped building. Because somewhere around week 3 of silence, going quiet started to feel easier than explaining what I was actually doing.

Last night, at 12:17am, I found out why that silence mattered more than I thought.

I'm building Zealova, an AI fitness coach, solo. One of the ugliest, least demoable parts of that job is exercise data integrity. A workout schedule shows a name. Tapping it opens an exercise. Those two things are supposed to be the same exercise. They are not always.

I found the deepest version of that bug last night. A chunk of our exercise library, real exercises with real verified video, had never been linked into the system that decides what actually plays when a user taps in. So the app quietly fell back to the closest existing match instead. Five different foam rolling exercises for five different body parts, all playing the same video. A wall ball exercise resolving to a totally different move.

None of that shows up in a demo. Nobody claps for a data integrity fix. But it is the difference between a user trusting the app's plan and a user quietly losing that trust the third time the wrong exercise shows up.

I wrote a script that fixed it in one pass, dry run by default, and set up so it self heals every future program automatically, not just this one.

That was one bug, from one night. Over the 7 weeks I was not posting here, I shipped 872 commits solo. Most of them are exactly this kind of invisible correctness work.

I am back to narrating the process, not just the wins. If you build anything solo, I would guess you know the version of this where the quiet stretch is actually the most productive one, and also the hardest one to talk about.

What is the least demoable but most important thing you have shipped recently?
