## Shot list — Outreach demo clip (version 1, 2026-05-18)

**Purpose:** attach to listicle and press pitches as a product walkthrough. First use: Fortune "Best Workout Apps" pitch to Christina Snyder.

**Target runtime:** 60-90 seconds total.

**Platform recommendation: record on iOS Simulator, not the Android emulator.**

Reason: iOS Simulator produces a cleaner, higher-resolution output at 1x/2x/3x logical pixel density with no device-chrome artifacts. Android emulator often shows system-bar flicker, status-bar notification noise, and emulator chrome that reads as unpolished in a press context. The actual shipping platform is Android (Play Store is live), but journalists reviewing for listicles are almost always on iPhones — the iOS UI will feel immediately familiar. iOS App Store review is in progress, so the iOS build is real. Record on iOS Simulator at iPhone 15 Pro dimensions (393x852 pt, 3x retina). Export at 1080x2340 or crop to 1080x1920 for a clean 9:16 aspect, or keep it as a native vertical screen recording for email attachment — both work.

**No audio needed.** This is a press clip, not a social Reel. A journalist attaches AirPods in a noisy office or watches it muted on a second monitor. The UI tells the story. If you want to add anything in post: one track of very quiet ambient music (not fitness-genre pump-up, something neutral like a lo-fi piano or ambient pad at 20% volume). Do not add voiceover — it ages out of date when features change.

### Recommended shot order (journalist reads best in this sequence)

The logic: open with the biggest capability claim (AI plan generation), follow with the thing that makes daily use sticky (food logging), add the differentiator that surprises (menu scan), close with the moat (multi-agent chat). This mirrors how journalists structure comparison tables: headline feature first, daily-use features in the middle, unique feature last as the reason to pick this over the others.

### Shot 1 — AI workout plan generation (the headline)

**Starts at:** 0:00
**Ends at:** approx 0:18
**Hold duration per sub-step:** 2-3 seconds each

**Navigation path:**
1. App opens on the Home tab. Hold on the home dashboard for 2 seconds so the journalist registers what the app is (a fitness app, not a utility).
2. Tap the Workouts tab (second bottom-nav icon).
3. Scroll down to find the current month's plan or the AI-generated plan section. Hold for 2 seconds so the structure is visible: days of the week, muscle groups, named exercises.
4. Tap on today's workout (or any day's workout) to open the workout detail screen. Hold for 3 seconds. The journalist needs to see: exercise names, sets/reps, progression. This is the "progressive overload" criterion Fortune uses.
5. Tap on a single exercise to show the exercise detail screen (illustration or image, muscle group, form cue). Hold 2 seconds.
6. Tap Back to return to the workout detail.

**On-screen text overlay (post-production, optional):**
- At step 3, add a small label in the lower-left corner: "AI-generated monthly plan" in a clean sans-serif at 70% opacity white. Not a loud callout, just a caption.
- At step 4, add: "Adapts weekly based on completion"

**What the journalist sees:** a fully populated plan with real exercises, sets, reps, and muscle group logic. No mock data, no placeholder text. The plan looks like something a real coach wrote. That's the demo moment.

### Shot 2 — Food photo logging

**Starts at:** approx 0:18
**Ends at:** approx 0:40
**Hold duration per sub-step:** 2-3 seconds each

**Navigation path:**
1. Tap the Nutrition tab (third bottom-nav icon).
2. The nutrition diary loads. Hold 2 seconds — the journalist sees the macro tracker at the top and the meal log below.
3. Tap the camera/log button (the food photo logging entry point). The camera or image picker launches.
4. Either: pick a pre-saved food photo from the simulator's photo library (set this up before recording — import a clean photo of a plated meal like a grilled chicken bowl or similar). Do not use a blurry photo; stage this.
5. After selecting the photo: the AI analysis result screen loads showing extracted food items, calorie estimates, and macros per item. Hold this screen for 4 seconds. This is the money shot for food logging. The journalist needs to read at least 2-3 food items and their calorie counts.
6. Tap "Log" or confirm to add to the diary. Hold 1 second on the confirmation.
7. Briefly return to the nutrition diary to show the entry now logged. Hold 2 seconds.

**On-screen text overlay (post-production, optional):**
- Over the analysis result screen: "Photo → macros in seconds"
- Small label: "Identifies individual items, not just total calories"

**What the journalist sees:** the food photo flow end-to-end. No typing, no barcode scan, no manual search. Photo in, diary entry out. This is the "show, don't tell" moment for the calorie logging claim.

**Staging note:** Before recording, go to the iOS Simulator's Photos app and import 2-3 clean food photos. A grilled chicken plate, a salad, or a meal from a recognizable restaurant all work. Avoid cluttered photos or low-light shots. The AI result screen needs to be readable at 1080p resolution.

### Shot 3 — Restaurant menu scan

**Starts at:** approx 0:40
**Ends at:** approx 0:58
**Hold duration per sub-step:** 2-3 seconds each

**Navigation path:**
1. Stay in the Nutrition tab. Tap the camera/log button again or navigate to the menu scan entry point (analysis mode: "menu").
2. Select a pre-saved photo of a restaurant menu from the simulator photo library. Stage this before recording: grab a screenshot of any real restaurant's menu page (Chipotle, Sweetgreen, or similar common chain). Crop it cleanly and import to the simulator.
3. The menu scan result screen loads showing dishes extracted from the menu, with calorie and macro estimates per dish. Hold for 4 seconds. The journalist needs to read 3-5 menu items with their calorie counts.
4. Optionally tap one dish to show the detail (macros breakdown). Hold 2 seconds.
5. Log one dish. Hold 1 second on confirmation.

**On-screen text overlay (post-production, optional):**
- Over the menu result screen: "Scan any restaurant menu"
- Small label: "Calorie estimates per dish, no typing"

**Why this shot matters (note for whoever edits the clip):** Most calorie apps break down at restaurants. MFP requires you to search by restaurant name + dish name and hope it's in their database. Cal AI handles food photos but not menu photos. Zealova reads the menu directly. This 18-second sequence is the single highest-differentiation moment in the entire demo. Do not cut it short.

### Shot 4 — Multi-agent chat coach

**Starts at:** approx 0:58
**Ends at:** approx 1:18 (keeping under 90 sec total)
**Hold duration per sub-step:** 2-3 seconds each

**Navigation path:**
1. Navigate to the Chat tab or wherever the AI coach chat lives in the nav. If it's accessible from the home screen quick-actions, use that path — it's faster.
2. The chat interface loads with conversation history visible (or empty if first launch). Hold 2 seconds.
3. Show one pre-typed exchange already in the chat history OR type a short message live during the recording. Recommended: stage a conversation thread before recording. Example exchange to stage:
   - User: "Can you swap my Thursday leg day? My knee is sore."
   - Coach (Injury agent): "Sure. I've replaced the barbell squats with leg press and the lunges with seated leg extensions to take the knee out of deep flexion. Thursday is updated."
   - User: "Also what should I eat to help recovery?"
   - Nutrition agent: "For joint recovery, prioritize protein (0.7-1g per lb bodyweight) and add omega-3s. A salmon and quinoa bowl tonight would cover both. Want me to log it?"
4. Scroll through this staged conversation slowly so the journalist reads both messages. Hold 4-5 seconds on the exchange.
5. End on the chat input field with the cursor blinking. This gives a natural "the conversation continues" feeling.

**On-screen text overlay (post-production, optional):**
- In the upper left of the chat screen: "5 specialist agents — workout, nutrition, injury, hydration, coach"
- Optionally: a small "Powered by Google Gemini" badge if the app shows that, or just skip it.

**What the journalist sees:** one question answered by two different specialist agents in one conversation thread. No switching apps, no switching modes. The injury agent reroutes the workout; the nutrition agent follows up. That's the multi-agent moat in 20 seconds.

**Staging note:** Stage the conversation before recording. Go into the app on the simulator, type those messages live (or a version of them), let the agents respond naturally, then start the screen recording from a scrolled-up position so you can slowly scroll down to reveal the exchange during the take.
